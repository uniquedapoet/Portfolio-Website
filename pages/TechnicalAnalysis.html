About the Project

This Stock Technical Analysis project was developed to enhance my expertise in financial analysis and data visualization. The project involves a detailed examination of stock data, followed by the creation of interactive dashboards that provide insights into stock performance, trends, and technical indicators. Full implementation is available in a Notebook on Kaggle. [Check it out here]

The dataset was sourced from Yahoo Finance and includes various stock attributes such as open, high, low, close, and volume. After cleaning and preprocessing the data for accuracy and consistency, I conducted an exploratory data analysis (EDA) to identify patterns and correlations. I also added many technical indicators such as moving averages, RSI, MACD, and Bollinger Bands, in an effort to prepare the data for the use in a Machine Learning Model.

Throughout this project I was able to learn a lot of new concepts in the financial analysis field, such as the importance of technical indicators, how to interpret them, and how to use them to make informed decisions.

Features

  • The top chart shows the closing price of the stock, while the bottom chart shows the moving average convergence divergence (MACD) indicator and its signal line. The MACD is a trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of a stocks price. This chart is color-coded to allow users to easily identify buy and sell signals.
  • The chart was created using Plotly and provides an interactive experience for users to explore the data.
  • This chart shows the Relative Strength Index (RSI) indicator, which measures the speed and change of price movements. The RSI ranges from 0 to 100 and is typically used to identify overbought or oversold conditions in a stock. When the stock price dips below the green line, it is considered oversold, and when it rises above the red line, it is considered overbought.
  • This is a candlestick chart that shows the open, high, low, and close prices of the stock. The chart is color-coded to allow users to easily identify bullish and bearish patterns. Also present is the volume of the stock traded on that day and the support and resistance levels.
